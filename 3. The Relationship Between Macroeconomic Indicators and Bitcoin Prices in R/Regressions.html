Effect of adding lagged log(Bitcoin_Prices) is surprising - adjusted R-squared equals 1, showing near perfect fit. That means, that prices of Bitcoin from the past have far stronger influence on today prices than any other variable. To adress that problem, the structure of the model is to be changed to First Differences, allowing to explore the relationship between other variables and Bitcoin_Prices.

Collinearity test - Variation Inflation Factor

vif(model2)
## log(Gold_Price)        OECD_CPI      log(WM2NS)             VIX 
##        1.930988        2.063353        2.506482        1.204473
vif(model_trend)
## log(Gold_Price)        OECD_CPI      log(WM2NS)             VIX           trend 
##        2.332502        2.063472       33.994328        1.219123       27.801238

Variation Inflation Factor shows values significantly higher than 10 for trend and log(WM2NS) in model with time trend. This shows high collinearity with global liquidity (log(WM2NS)), what implies that much of time trend is already included in liquidity. Analysis of time plot of global liquidity supports this conclusion. Therefore trend is not to be included and another method to include time trend is to be used.
